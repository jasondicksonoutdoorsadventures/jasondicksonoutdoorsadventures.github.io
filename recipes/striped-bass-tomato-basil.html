Striped Bass with Tomato and Basil Sauce

Fresh from the water to the table

Striped bass with tomato and basil sauce is a light, fresh dish that highlights the natural flavor of the fish. The simple sauce adds brightness and balance without overpowering the meat.

One of the best rewards after a good day on the water is bringing that catch home and turning it into a meal worth sharing. This recipe keeps things simple with tomatoes, garlic, basil, lemon, and a little olive oil so the striped bass stays tender, flaky, and full of flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. striped bass fillets
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz.) diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add garlic and cook for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  2. Stir in diced tomatoes and tomato paste. Simmer for 10–15 minutes until slightly thickened.
  3. Add basil, salt, pepper, and lemon juice. Stir to combine.
  4. Season striped bass fillets lightly with salt and pepper.
  5. Place fish in the skillet and spoon sauce over the top.
  6. Cover and cook for 8–10 minutes until the fish is cooked through and flakes easily.
  7. Remove from heat and let rest briefly before serving.

Serve

Serve with rice, pasta, or crusty bread.

Tips

  • Fresh basil adds the best flavor.
  • Do not overcook the fish. It should remain tender and flaky.
  • Add a splash of white wine to the sauce for extra depth.
