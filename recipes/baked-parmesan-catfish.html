Baked Parmesan Catfish

Nothing beats bringing home fresh catfish after a successful day on the water. While fried catfish is a longtime favorite, this baked version offers a lighter alternative without sacrificing flavor. A crispy Parmesan and breadcrumb coating keeps every bite tender and flaky inside. It is quick to prepare, easy to make, and perfect for a family meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds catfish fillets
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or melted butter
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped, optional
  • Lemon wedges for serving, optional

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
  2. Lightly grease a baking dish or line it with parchment paper.
  3. Pat the catfish fillets dry with paper towels.
  4. In a bowl, combine the Parmesan cheese, breadcrumbs, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, black pepper, and salt.
  5. Brush both sides of each fillet with olive oil or melted butter.
  6. Coat each fillet evenly with the Parmesan mixture, pressing gently so the coating sticks.
  7. Arrange the fillets in the prepared baking dish.
  8. Bake for 15 to 20 minutes, or until the fish flakes easily with a fork and the coating is golden brown.
  9. For extra crispiness, broil the fish for the final 2 to 3 minutes. Watch it closely to prevent burning.
  10. Garnish with chopped parsley and serve with lemon wedges, if desired.

Serving Suggestions

  • Roasted potatoes
  • French fries
  • Coleslaw
  • Steamed vegetables
  • A fresh garden salad
  • Hush puppies

Tips

  • Do not overcook the catfish. It should remain moist and flaky.
  • Freshly grated Parmesan provides the best flavor and texture.
  • A squeeze of fresh lemon juice adds a bright finishing touch.
  • Cooking time may vary slightly depending on the thickness of the fillets.
