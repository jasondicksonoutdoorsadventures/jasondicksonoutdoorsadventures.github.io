The Strangest Hunt of All Time

I've had shotguns jam. I've had shells misfire. But I'd never seen an entire shotgun barrel fly into the lake.

The morning air was crisp as my buddy and I headed across the icy lake to our blind. The decoys were already set, and today we were excited to try our new jerk lines...

First Light

As daylight spread across the lake, we worked the jerk lines and watched the decoys come alive. Three teal committed to the spread, and we dropped all three to start the morning.

The Jerk Lines Worked

Later a cautious mallard circled our spread. A tug on the jerk line and a comeback call convinced him to finish. One shot later, we knew the jerk lines were worth every bit of effort.

Then Everything Went Wrong

A flock of gadwalls finally committed. I dropped two birds. My buddy fired once, pumped his shotgun...

...and the entire barrel flew off and splashed into the lake.

For a few seconds we simply stared. Then I couldn't help it—I started laughing. My buddy stood there holding a barrel-less shotgun, wondering what had just happened.

Lessons Learned

We packed up early with one working shotgun and one unbelievable story. To this day I believe a loose retaining nut caused the failure. The lesson is simple: always check your gear before heading out. You never know what surprises the day might hold.
