Chasing Yellowfin in Venice

A Louisiana offshore fishing trip to remember

Some fishing trips are fun. Others become memories you'll tell for the rest of your life. Our trip to Venice, Louisiana, was definitely one of those.

The adventure began with a flight into New Orleans, where I met my good friend Leslie. Since I arrived the day before our charter, we decided to spend the evening exploring the famous French Quarter. We walked the historic streets, enjoyed the sights and sounds of the city, and soaked in everything that makes New Orleans such a unique place.

The next morning, we picked up Leslie's friend, Teresa, from the airport. With everyone finally together, we loaded our gear and made our way south toward Venice, Louisiana, one of the best places in the Gulf for offshore fishing.

After checking into the hotel, we settled into the room and got everything ready for the next day. The plan was simple: get some rest, wake up early, and be ready for a full day chasing tuna.

At 4:30 the next morning, the alarm went off. We got up, gathered our gear, and headed to Cypress Cove Marina to meet Captain Christian, who would be taking us out. By 5:30, we met our captain and first mate, loaded everything into the boat, and headed out as daylight started to break.

Our first stop was for bait. Captain Christian threw the cast net, but we didn't catch many. So we moved farther out toward the shallow rigs, where we started fishing to catch our bait. Leslie and Teresa did most of the bait catching while I stayed busy scooping up the bait and putting it into the tank.

Once we had bait, we left the shallow rigs behind and made the run toward the deep-water rigs. That's where the real fishing began.

Tuna Fishing Adventure

When we got to the rigs, we found fish. Captain Christian set the first line out, and it didn't take long before we were hooked up. Leslie was first up. She reeled and reeled, fighting that fish hard, and within about seven minutes she had it to the top.

That first yellowfin tuna weighed around 80 pounds.

The next fish was Teresa's. In fact, Teresa and I both hooked up at the same time. Two fish on, two rods bent, and everyone working together. We fought them side by side and ended up landing both fish. Each one weighed around 70 pounds.

After that, the action stayed hot. We ended up limiting out in about an hour, with two fish apiece. One of the tuna weighed well over 100 pounds.

It was the kind of trip every angler hopes for: good friends, a great captain and crew, beautiful water, hard-fighting fish, and memories that will last a lifetime.

Some trips are measured by the number of fish you catch. Others are measured by the memories you bring home. This trip gave us both.

— Jason Dickson
