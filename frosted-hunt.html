The Frosted Hunt

A January Morning to Remember

It was a cold January morning, the kind that leaves your breath hanging in the air like smoke and turns your boots to ice. Four of us, bundled in layers, shivered as we made our way to the blind before dawn.

The temperature sat stubbornly at fifteen degrees with a steady south wind. Facing northwest, our blind overlooked rippling water—the perfect setup for a memorable duck hunt.

By legal shooting light the lake had begun to seal itself with thin sheets of ice. Four teal splashed into the decoys without us noticing. At the whispered count of “One... Two... Three!” we rose together. Three teal fell, and hot coffee never tasted better.

Not long afterward two bluebills committed perfectly to the decoys. Later, a flock of geese circled three times before locking their wings over the spread. At the signal we stood together, and the morning erupted into honks, feathers, and six geese on the water.

By ten o’clock three mallards finished the hunt. As we packed the decoys and headed for the ramp, four ring-necked ducks appeared overhead—just enough to remind us there is always one more opportunity waiting for the next hunt.

We left the frozen lake with another unforgettable morning of friendship, frost, and memories that will last a lifetime.

— Jason Dickson
