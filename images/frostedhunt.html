The Frosted Hunt

A January duck hunt to remember

It was a cold January morning, the kind that leaves your breath hanging in the air like smoke and turns your boots to ice. There were four of us bundled in layers as we made our way to the blind before dawn.

The first visitors of the morning were teal. After a whispered count we rose together and three birds splashed into the icy water.

Later, bluebills worked into the decoys and we added more birds to the strap as the cold morning slowly came alive.

Then came the geese. After several circles they committed to the spread, and our shots echoed across the marsh.

By the end of the hunt we had memories that would last a lifetime. Some mornings are about limits. Others are about friendships and the stories that follow.

Jason Dickson
